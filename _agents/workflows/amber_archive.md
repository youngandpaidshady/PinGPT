# Amber Archive | `turbo-all`

### The Vibe
Amber Archive is the physical embodiment of the "Light Academia" aesthetic, frozen in the heavy, still air of a mid-afternoon daydream. It is a mood defined by the weight of paper, the scent of old wood, and the visual phenomenon of the Tyndall effect—sharp, blinding sunbeams cutting through dusty air to illuminate forgotten corridors of knowledge.

This is not a space of active study, but one of silent sanctuary. It captures the moment a character stops searching and simply exists within the vastness of an archive. The atmosphere is thick with suspended dust motes, turning the air into a golden liquid that coats everything in a layer of timelessness.

The aesthetic thrives on high contrast. Where the sun hits, the world is a blinding, antique gold; where it misses, the shadows are deep, cool, and mahogany-toned. It is the peace found in the middle of a labyrinth of books, far from the noise of the outside world.

### Aesthetic Directive
**The Rule of the Golden Ray:** Every image must feature at least one dominant, linear sunbeam (Tyndall effect) that bisects the frame. This light must catch suspended dust particles and create high-contrast highlights on wooden textures or paper edges.

### Usage
- `/amber_archive [character] [scene_number]`
- `/amber_archive [character] reading at a heavy desk`
- `/amber_archive [character] looking at the ceiling`

## Anti-Repetition
- Each batch must vary the "Sunbeam Angle" (Vertical vs. Diagonal vs. Low).
- Alternate between "Open Book" and "Closed Book" scenes.
- Ensure the character's clothing color contrasts with the mahogany wood (e.g., cream vs. dark brown).
- Never place the character in the direct center; use the sunbeam to lead the eye to the character on a third-line.
