# /burnoutdesk — The Overachiever's Crash

// turbo-all

## The Vibe

**He ran at 100% until the body said no.** Birds-eye view. A desk covered in papers, textbooks stacked like barricades, crumpled paper balls like grenades that didn't work. And him — face-down on his notes, pen still in hand, tie still on, lamp still burning. The body crashed before the mind let go. This is what ambition looks like after midnight: not glory, not inspiration — **collapse**.

This is a **performance-crash niche** — the visual of a strong/capable/driven character rendered powerless by their own work ethic. The birds-eye camera angle is the SECRET WEAPON: it creates a voyeuristic "looking down on someone at their most unguarded" emotional hit that NO other PinGPT mood uses. The audience feels like they're witnessing something private — a failure that was never meant to be seen.

> **🚨 CAMERA ANGLE CRITICAL! 🚨**
> At least 60% of prompts MUST use overhead/birds-eye angle. This is the compositional signature. The remaining 40% can use profile or three-quarter desk views, but the overhead is what makes this mood distinct.

> **🚨 AESTHETIC DIRECTIVE: NO 2.5D BLENDING! 🚨**
> Character = flat 2D anime cel-shading. Desk/room/props = richly detailed atmospheric. Lamp light, paper textures, crumpled balls = atmospheric.

## Usage

```
/burnoutdesk nanami 5     → 5 burnout desk prompts for Nanami
/burnoutdesk any 8        → 8 prompts rotating characters
```
