# Fumikiri Red Pulse (fumikiri_red_pulse) // turbo-all

## The Vibe
The 'Fumikiri' (rail crossing) is the heartbeat of urban melancholy. It is the rhythmic 'ding-ding-ding' of the warning bell that signals a forced pause in the world’s momentum. While the express train blurs past in a streak of white and yellow, the character remains static, washed in the alternating pulse of signal red and the deep, heavy indigo of the blue hour.

This mood captures the feeling of being caught between destinations—the "on hold" state of existence. It is high-contrast, moody, and cinematic, using the industrial geometry of train tracks and crossing bars to frame a solitary figure waiting for a signal that hasn't changed yet.

## Aesthetic Directive
**Primary Visual Rule:** Every scene must feature a rhythmic red light source (signal lamp, warning LED, or tail light) contrasting against a dominant Midnight Blue/Charcoal environment. Use motion blur on background elements (trains) to emphasize the character's stillness.

## Anti-Repetition
- **NO Cyberpunk**: Do not use holograms, neon-soaked cities, or futuristic tech.
- **NO Emotion Words**: Do not use "sad," "lonely," or "depressed." Use "looking down," "hands in pockets," or "eyes shadowed."
- **Dual Lighting Only**: Every prompt must balance a cold light (Blue Hour) with a warm/red light (Signal).
- **Static Character**: Subject must be crisp and clear, background must have motion blur (1/15 shutter feel).
