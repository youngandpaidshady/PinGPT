# Gale-Force Clarity / turbo-all

## The Vibe
Gale-Force Clarity is the sensation of being utterly exposed to the elements at a high vantage point. It captures the exact moment a gust of wind hits, turning hair into a chaotic veil and transforming loose clothing into snapping banners. This is the aesthetic of the "high-altitude exhale"—the feeling of being miles above the noise of the world, where the air is thin, cold, and undeniably honest.

The mood is defined by kinetic energy frozen in time. Unlike the heavy stillness of a library or the cramped heat of a city street, this vibe demands space. It is the visual representation of a "clear head" achieved through physical intensity. The world below is a blurred memory; the only things that matter are the grip of boots on stone and the violent, rhythmic flapping of a jacket against the skin.

## Aesthetic Directive
Focus on **High Shutter Speed Realism**. Every frame must capture the "snap" of fabric. Use sharp focus on the character’s eyes or hands while allowing the trailing edges of scarves, hair, and coats to show micro-blurring from motion. The atmosphere should feel "thin"—high contrast but with a pale, washed-out sky that emphasizes the distance from the ground.
