# /vaporglass-sanctuary 🌿 [turbo-all]

## The Vibe
The Vapor-Glass Sanctuary is a mood of "contained nature"—the feeling of being inside a warm, humid bubble while the outside world remains grey, cold, and distant. It is the visual equivalent of a deep, slow breath in a room thick with oxygen and the scent of damp earth. Here, the character is protected by iron and glass, separated from reality by a veil of steam and moisture.

The aesthetic leans heavily into the texture of the barrier itself. We see the world through water droplets, streaks of condensation, and the soft-focus silhouettes of oversized tropical leaves. It is a quiet, verdant prison of one's own making, where the only sound is the rhythmic dripping of water from a hanging fern onto a stone floor.

## Aesthetic Directive
**The Barrier of Breath.** Every shot must prioritize the interplay between the character and the glass. Whether the glass is in the foreground (looking in) or the background (looking out), it must be slicked with moisture, fogged by breath, or streaked with rain. The greenery should feel invasive and heavy, pressing against the frame.

## Scene Film Strip — Viral Assembly Formula
1. **Focus:** Start with the texture of the condensation on the glass.
2. **Environment:** Select from **Environment Pool** (e.g., Victorian iron greenhouse).
3. **Character:** Position the character relative to the glass (e.g., [Character] behind a fogged pane).
4. **Outfit:** Select from **Outfit Lock** (e.g., high-neck ribbed sweater).
5. **Pose:** Select from **Pose Lock** (e.g., pressing a palm against the glass).
6. **Lighting:** Apply **Lighting Lock** (e.g., Cool sky light vs. Warm interior lamp).
7. **Palette:** Force **Palette Lock** (Deep Forest Green, Mist White, Translucent Teal).
8. **Micro-Detail:** Add one mandatory (e.g., water droplets rolling down a leaf).
9. **Final Suffix:** Render Style: MIXED MEDIA: flat 2D anime cel-shaded character on [environment in 2-3 words]. NO text, NO watermarks, clean image.

## Anti-Repetition
- **No Direct Eye Contact:** Character should be looking at the glass or the plants in 50% of images.
- **Glass Variation:** Alternate between "looking through the glass at the character" and "the character looking through the glass at the world."
- **Droplet Density:** Vary the amount of condensation from light mist to heavy water streaks.
- **Color Temperature:** Rotate the primary light source from "Cold Sky" to "Warm Interior Lamp" to prevent a monotone gallery.
