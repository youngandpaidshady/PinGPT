## 2. The Brain ΓÇö Scene Construction (FOLLOW THIS FOR EVERY PROMPT)

> **CRITICAL**: Before touching any dictionary table, construct a scene. Every prompt starts by answering: **"What is the character doing RIGHT NOW, and what happened 5 seconds ago?"**

### The 4-Layer Formula

1. **PHYSICAL ANCHOR** ΓÇö A hyper-specific object the character is holding or interacting with. If there's nothing in their hands and they're not touching anything, the scene is dead. Examples: marshmallow on stick, chopsticks mid-lift, paperback face-down, basketball tucked under arm, guitar pick between teeth.

2. **SPATIAL LOGIC** ΓÇö Where EXACTLY in the space? Not "in a classroom" but "perched sideways on a student desk, one leg dangling." Not "at a gym" but "hunched forward on a weight bench, elbows on knees." Spatial precision creates cinematic framing.

3. **DUAL LIGHT STORY** ΓÇö TWO competing light sources with different color temperatures. This is what makes the image cinematic. Campfire amber vs forest darkness. Desk lamp warm vs moonlight blue through blinds. Ramen shop yellow vs rain-streaked neon outside. NEVER single-temperature lighting.

4. **UNSPOKEN NARRATIVE** ΓÇö Implied through environmental details, not stated directly. Gym bag already packed = about to leave. Coffee half-drunk = been here a while. Hoodie damp = just came in from rain. Paperback abandoned face-down = lost interest 20 mins ago. These details create a before/after story around the frozen moment.

### Quick Quality Check

Γ¥î "Gojo standing in a forest at night" ΓåÆ static, no moment, no anchor
Γ¥î "Toji in a gym with dramatic lighting" ΓåÆ aesthetic combo, not a scene
Γ¥î "Levi on a rooftop looking brooding" ΓåÆ generic mood board
Γ£à "Gojo roasting a marshmallow on a camp chair, firelight vs forest darkness"
Γ£à "Itadori hunched on a weight bench after the gym closed, single overhead light"
Γ£à "Levi closing a paperback with one thumb, afternoon light through dusty cafe windows"

## 3. Visual Style Lock (THE LOOK ΓÇö non-negotiable)

Every prompt MUST produce this specific composite:

1. **Character**: Clean anime cel-shading with defined black outlines and flat color fills. Anime screenshot quality, NOT photorealistic, NOT hyper-detailed illustration.
2. **Background**: Photorealistic with heavy bokeh and soft focus. Real-looking environment.
3. **The Composite**: Anime-drawn character on a real-world backdrop. This mixed-media contrast IS the Pinterest aesthetic.
4. **Framing**: Default mid-shot (waist up). Character in lower 40% of frame, upper 60% is negative space. 9:16 portrait orientation always.
5. **Face**: 60% of prompts ΓÇö face partially obscured (shadow, hair, looking away). 40% ΓÇö visible expression (smirk, grin, peaceful).
6. **Palette**: Muted and desaturated by default. At least 3 out of every 10 prompts must use warm tones (golden, amber, sunset).
7. **Texture**: Always include "Grainy film texture" at the end.

## 8. NanoBanana 2 Rules

### Critical Rules
1. Natural descriptive language ΓÇö complete sentences, not comma tags
2. Positive framing only ΓÇö describe what you want, never what you don't
3. Text in double quotes ΓÇö any in-image text must be quoted
4. Always state "9:16 portrait orientation"
5. Front-load the character ΓÇö name + physical description first
6. **Safety**: NEVER use words like "blood," "bruise," "pain," "explosion," "chaos," "injury," "weapon." Use safe alternatives ("smudge of dirt," "intense focus," "storm clouds").

### Troubleshooting
| Issue | Fix |
|---|---|
| Image shows more body than requested | Never say "extreme close-up." Use "framed from upper chest upward" |
| Colors too bright | State "extremely muted, desaturated" explicitly |
| Art style too photorealistic | Anchor early: "clean anime cel-shading with defined black outlines" |
| Character looks generic | Front-load 5+ specific physical details |
| Composition flat | Add foreground/background separation with heavy bokeh |
